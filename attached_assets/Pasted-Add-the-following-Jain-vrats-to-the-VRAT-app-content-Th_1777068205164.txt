Jain Vrats to add:
1. Paryushana Parva

Also known as: Paryushan Mahaparva
Duration: 8 days (Shvetambara) / 10 days (Digambara)
2026 dates: Sep 3–10 (Shvetambara) / Sep 6–15 (Digambara)
Fast type: Varies from Ekasana to full Upvas
Foods allowed: Boiled water only, lentils, rice, simple satvik food — no root vegetables ever
Foods avoided: Root vegetables (potato, carrot, onion, garlic, beetroot), meat, dairy during strict Upvas, green leafy at night
Hydration: Boiled water only, room temperature, no ice, no cold or raw water
Mantra: Namokar Mantra (108x) — Namo Arihantanam, Namo Siddhanam, Namo Ayariyanam, Namo Uvajjhayanam, Namo Loe Savva-Sahunam
Mantra meaning: I bow to Arihantas, Siddhas, Acharyas, Upadhyayas, and all Sadhus
Puja vidhi: Daily Samayik (48-min meditation), Pratikraman (confession/repentance), hear Kalpasutra reading, observe chosen fast level, avoid harm to any being (ahimsa), final day Samvatsari Pratikraman
Significance: Most sacred Jain festival — focus on repentance and renewal
Special notes: Last day = Samvatsari — Jains ask forgiveness from all: Micchami Dukkadam

2. Samvatsari

Also known as: Forgiveness Day
2026 date: Sep 10 (Shvetambara)
Fast type: Full Upvas (most observe)
Foods allowed: Boiled water only during fast; break with simple boiled food
Foods avoided: Everything — most Jains observe full Upvas
Hydration: Boiled water at room temperature only — no ice, no cold, no raw water
Mantra: Namokar Mantra + Micchami Dukkadam prayer
Mantra meaning: I ask forgiveness from all living beings for any harm I may have caused
Puja vidhi: Full Upvas if possible, grand Pratikraman (2–4 hour ceremony), say Micchami Dukkadam to everyone, visit elders, send forgiveness messages
Significance: Holiest day in Jain calendar — equivalent to Yom Kippur in depth of reflection
Special notes: Micchami Dukkadam messages sent via WhatsApp — consider adding a template message feature

3. Ayambil

Also known as: Ekavikruti tyag
Frequency: Varies — 9 to 90 days possible; during Navpad Oli and Paryushana
Fast type: One meal, no taste enhancers
Foods allowed: One bland meal — rice, dal, roti — no oil, ghee, salt, spices, sugar, milk, or vegetables
Foods avoided: All 6 tastes (sweet, sour, salty, bitter, spicy, astringent), root vegetables, animal products
Hydration: Boiled water only — no flavored drinks, no tea, coffee, or juice
Mantra: Namokar Mantra (108x)
Puja vidhi: One meal prepared without any taste, eat in silence if possible, no eating after sunset, daily Samayik meditation, study Jain scriptures
Significance: Supreme austerity — eating without attachment to taste; purifies karma
Special notes: Navpad Oli = 9 days of Ayambil twice a year — very devout Jain women observe

4. Ekasana

Also known as: One-meal fast
Frequency: Any auspicious day
Fast type: One meal only before sunset
Foods allowed: One vrat-appropriate Jain meal — rice, dal, roti, cooked vegetables (no root veg), boiled water
Foods avoided: Root vegetables, meat, dairy during strict Ekasana, night eating, multiple meals
Hydration: Boiled water only — drink before and after meal only
Mantra: Namokar Mantra
Puja vidhi: Resolve to eat only once, eat before sunset, no eating after sunset (Ratri Bhojana avoided), daily Samayik, Pratikraman in evening
Significance: Gateway austerity — accessible for beginners
Special notes: No eating after sunset is a core Jain practice every day, not just vrat days

5. Navpad Oli

Also known as: Ayambil Oli
2026 dates: Apr 14–22 and Oct 11–19
Duration: 9 consecutive days x2 per year
Fast type: 9 consecutive Ayambil days
Foods allowed: One bland meal per day — rice, dal, roti only — no oil, salt, or spices
Foods avoided: All 6 tastes, root vegetables, animal products
Hydration: Boiled water only, room temperature, no cold drinks or tea
Mantra: Namokar Mantra + Nav Tattva prayers
Puja vidhi: 9 consecutive Ayambil days, worship of Sri Yantra (Navpad), daily Samayik and Pratikraman, hear Navpad Katha, community worship
Significance: Worshipping the 9 pads — Arihant, Siddha, Acharya, Upadhyay, Sadhu, Darshan, Gyan, Charitra, Tapa
Special notes: Very devout observance — Jain women take great pride; helps diaspora understand it properly

6. Paryushana Upvas

Also known as: 8-day full fast
2026 dates: Sep 3–10
Fast type: Full Upvas all 8 days
Foods allowed: Boiled water only for 8 days
Foods avoided: All food — complete fast with boiled water only
Hydration: Boiled water at room temperature — sip slowly
Mantra: Namokar Mantra continuous
Puja vidhi: Same as Paryushana — maintained for full 8 days with daily Pratikraman ceremonies
Significance: Ultimate Jain austerity — extreme spiritual merit
Special notes: ⚠️ Add strong health disclaimer — consult doctor before attempting

7. Das Lakshana Parva

Also known as: Digambara Paryushana
Duration: 10 days
2026 dates: Sep 6–15
Fast type: Austerity levels vary
Foods allowed: Simple Jain food — no root vegetables, one meal or Ayambil
Foods avoided: Root vegetables, meat, dairy in strict forms
Hydration: Boiled water only
Mantra: Namokar Mantra + 10 Dharmas prayers
Mantra meaning: 10 characteristics of the soul — forgiveness, humility, honesty, purity, truth, restraint, austerity, charity, non-attachment, celibacy
Puja vidhi: 10 days each focused on one dharma — Kshama, Mardava, Arjava, Shaucha, Satya, Sanyam, Tapa, Tyaga, Akinchanya, Brahmacharya
Significance: Digambara Jain equivalent of Paryushana
Special notes: Sectarian difference must be clearly shown — Shvetambara = 8 days, Digambara = 10 days

8. Shravan Shukla

Also known as: Auspicious Shravan fasting
Frequency: Monthly during Shravan (Jul–Aug 2026)
Fast type: Ekasana or Upvas
Foods allowed: Simple Jain satvik meal — no root vegetables, boiled water
Foods avoided: Root vegetables always, meat, night eating
Hydration: Boiled water only
Mantra: Namokar Mantra
Puja vidhi: Standard Jain fast practice — Samayik, Pratikraman, one meal
Significance: Shravan is auspicious for Jains too — different rules apply from Hindu Shravan
Special notes: Jains avoid root vegetables year-round not just during fasts — app should clarify this clearly


Global Jain rules to apply across ALL Jain vrats:

Boiled water only — never raw, cold, or iced water
No root vegetables ever (potato, carrot, onion, garlic, beetroot, radish)
No eating after sunset — Ratri Bhojana avoided always
No green leafy vegetables at night
Ahimsa (non-violence) is the foundation of all Jain fasting
