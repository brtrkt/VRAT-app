EKADASHI VRAT KATHA:
Once the sage Narada Muni asked Lord Vishnu — "O Lord, what is the greatest vrat a devotee can observe?" Lord Vishnu replied — "Narada, of all vrats Ekadashi is the most sacred. On this day a demon named Mura hid inside grains and pulses to corrupt the minds of humans. When devotees avoid grains on Ekadashi they starve this demon and protect their soul from impurity. I am most pleased by this fast above all others." Narada asked — "Who first observed this vrat?" Lord Vishnu said — "Long ago a king named Mandhata was troubled by drought in his kingdom for three years. His people were starving. He went to the forest seeking answers and met the sage Angiras who told him — Observe Ekadashi with full devotion, avoid all grains, stay awake through the night chanting my name. The king returned and observed Ekadashi with his entire kingdom. That very night rain fell, crops grew and prosperity returned. From that day Ekadashi became the most beloved fast of Vishnu's devotees." Lord Vishnu then declared — "Whoever observes Ekadashi with a pure heart — whether fully, partially or simply by avoiding grains — I consider them my most devoted bhakt. The merit of one Ekadashi equals donating gold, performing yagnas and bathing in all holy rivers. On Dwadashi morning break your fast at the parana time and offer water and tulsi to me. This fast purifies all past karmas and leads the soul to Vaikuntha."
Jai Ekadashi Mata Ki Jai. Om Namo Bhagavate Vasudevaya.
PRADOSH VRAT KATHA:
Long ago the gods and demons churned the cosmic ocean — the Samudra Manthan — to obtain Amrit, the nectar of immortality. During the churning a deadly poison called Halahala emerged first, so powerful it could destroy all creation. The gods ran to Lord Shiva in terror. Without hesitation Lord Shiva held the poison in his throat — turning his throat blue — earning him the name Neelakantha, the blue throated one. He saved all creation at great personal cost. The gods were so moved by his sacrifice that they gathered at twilight — the Pradosh Kaal — and sang his praises, offered prayers and danced in gratitude. Lord Shiva was so pleased by their devotion at this twilight hour that he declared — "Whoever worships me during Pradosh Kaal on the thirteenth lunar day of each fortnight I will fulfil their deepest desires and grant them liberation." From that day Pradosh Vrat began. Devotees who fast through the day and offer milk, water and Bilva leaves to the Shiva Lingam during the ninety minutes around sunset on Pradosh receive Shiva and Parvati's combined blessings. It is said that on this day Shiva and Parvati are in the most joyful and giving mood — dancing together on Mount Kailash — and any sincere prayer made at this time is heard immediately.
Om Namah Shivaya. Har Har Mahadev.

AMAVASYA VRAT KATHA:
In ancient times there was a devoted son named Shravan Kumar who carried his blind elderly parents on a wooden frame on his shoulders, taking them on pilgrimage across India. One evening near a forest King Dasharatha — father of Lord Rama — was practicing Shabdbhedi Baan, the art of shooting arrows by sound alone. He heard the sound of water filling at a river and thought it was an animal drinking. He released his arrow. The arrow struck Shravan Kumar who cried out in pain. When Dasharatha ran to him he found a devoted young man dying. Shravan Kumar's last request was — "Please take water to my parents nearby. They are blind, elderly and waiting for me." Dasharatha carried water to the grieving parents. When they learned their devoted son had been killed they cursed Dasharatha — "As we die from grief of losing our son, so shall you also die from grief of separation from your son." This came true when Lord Rama went to the forest and Dasharatha died of grief. Amavasya is the day we remember and honour our ancestors — our Pitru. On this day the boundary between the world of the living and the world of ancestors becomes thin. Offering water, sesame seeds and prayers to our ancestors on Amavasya frees them from suffering and brings their blessings to our family. Never neglect your elders. Never forget those who came before you. Their peace is your prosperity.
Om Pitru Devaya Namaha. Om Namo Bhagavate Vasudevaya.
SANKASHTI CHATURTHI VRAT KATHA:
Once all the gods held a race — whoever circled the entire universe first would receive a special modak, the favourite sweet of Lord Ganesha, as a prize. All the gods mounted their vehicles and sped off across the universe. Lord Ganesha looked at his vehicle — a small mouse — and smiled. Instead of racing around the universe he simply walked around his parents Lord Shiva and Goddess Parvati three times and bowed to them. When asked why he said — "My parents are my entire universe. Circling them is equal to circling all creation." Lord Shiva and Parvati were so moved by this wisdom and devotion that they declared Ganesha the winner. The gods returned exhausted from their cosmic race to find Ganesha already holding the modak prize. From that day it was established that Lord Ganesha must be worshipped first before any puja or new beginning — for he who honours his parents and his roots has already circled the entire universe. Sankashti Chaturthi is observed on the fourth day of the dark fortnight each month. Devotees fast through the day, worship Lord Ganesha, offer durva grass and modak, and break their fast only after sighting the moon in the evening. Lord Ganesha removes all obstacles — Sankashti means the remover of all troubles — and his blessings on this day clear the path for success in all endeavours.
Om Gan Ganapataye Namaha. Om Vakratundaya Hum.
